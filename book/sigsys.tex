\section{Continuous and discrete time signals.}
A signal is a function that conveys information about a phenomenon, and a continous-time signal changes continously with time, such as the electromagnetic waves that are transmitted or received by a wireless device. \par A discrete-time signal is a sequence of measurements that are typically made at uniform times, for example stock prices observed at fixed times. Thus, a discrete-time signal $x[n]$ and the corresponding continuous-time signal $x(t)$ are related by a sampling process:

\begin{equation}
\mbox{$x[n] = x(nTs) = x(t)|_{t=nT_s}$}
\end{equation}
